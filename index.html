Home value and sale

Enter the current market value of the home and an expected annual market growth rate. If you choose to sell the home, the projected home value is added to the selected person's non-registered account. If you choose to downsize, enter the current value of the replacement home. The planner projects both the current home and replacement home using the same home growth rate, invests only the projected difference, and keeps the replacement home in the projection.

Rates and assumptions

This is an educational calculator, not financial, tax, or investment advice. Inputs autosave in this browser and can also be exported/imported as JSON. Chart display can show before-tax or simplified after-tax income. Tax uses simplified 2026 federal and provincial/territorial brackets and basic personal amounts only. CPP/OAS start-age adjustments use month-aware federal percentage rules and assume the entered CPP/OAS amounts are age-65 baselines. OAS is increased by 10% from age 75 onward. In two-person mode, Person 2 receives a simplified survivor CPP amount equal to 50% of Person 1's adjusted CPP after Person 1 dies. Other annual income is included only between the selected start and end ages. Income requirement adjustments are applied from the selected person's selected age onward as exact dollar changes, with no inflation scaling. Lump sum additions/removals are one-time absolute dollar changes to the selected person's selected account and are not inflation adjusted. Month-aware lump sum additions/removals and home sale proceeds are applied in the exact selected projection month, before that month's investment growth and withdrawal calculations. RRSP/TFSA contributions, retirement withdrawals, RRIF minimums, and investment compounding are modelled monthly, then summarized annually. Mandatory RRIF minimum withdrawals are shown separately in light blue. Forced RRIF withdrawals above required income are moved to the non-registered account and continue to grow at the post-retirement return. Investment returns are reduced by inflation only in constant dollar view; inflation adjusted view uses nominal returns. Spousal RRSP/TFSA transfer is simplified: remaining registered and TFSA balances are assumed to transfer fully to the survivor when one person dies.

Retirement projection

Income is projected from the first retirement period through the last life expectancy, with age timing tracked internally in whole months so 0 months is a valid exact-birthday choice. Stacked bars start at the first retirement year. The red line shows the exact desired income after adjustments, while the dashed black line shows the baseline desired income before adjustments. Hovering over the chart shows total income, target income, surplus or shortfall, and tax context.
